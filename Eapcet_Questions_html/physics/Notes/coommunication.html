Communication System

Communication System

A **communication system** transmits information from one place to another using electrical signals.

✅ Basic Components:
  • Transmitter: Converts message signals into a suitable form for transmission.
  • Communication Channel: Medium through which the signal propagates (e.g., wired, wireless).
  • Receiver: Converts the received signal back into the original message.

Message Signals

Message signals carry information and can be classified into:

✅ Types of Signals:
  • Analog Signal: Continuous signals that vary smoothly over time.
  • Digital Signal: Discrete signals with only two values (0 and 1).

Need for Modulation

Modulation is the process of varying a carrier signal to transmit information efficiently.

✅ Reasons for Modulation:
  • To prevent **signal mixing** with noise.
  • To allow **long-distance transmission**.
  • To reduce **antenna size**.
  • To enable **multiplexing** (transmitting multiple signals simultaneously).

Types of Modulation

Modulation is classified based on how the carrier signal is varied:

✅ 1. Amplitude Modulation (AM)
  • Amplitude of carrier wave is varied in proportion to the message signal.
  • Equation: \( s(t) = [A + m(t)] \cos(\omega_c t) \)
  • Used in **AM radio broadcasting**.
✅ 2. Frequency Modulation (FM)
  • Frequency of carrier wave is varied according to the message signal.
  • Equation: \( s(t) = A \cos[\omega_c t + k_f \int m(t) dt] \)
  • Used in **FM radio, TV sound transmission**.

Demodulation

Demodulation is the process of extracting the original message signal from a modulated carrier wave.

✅ Importance of Demodulation:
  • Recovers the original transmitted signal.
  • Eliminates unwanted noise and distortion.
  • Used in **radios, televisions, and communication systems**.
✅ Types of Demodulation:
  • AM Demodulation: Uses an envelope detector to extract the amplitude variations.
  • FM Demodulation: Uses a frequency discriminator or phase-locked loop (PLL).

Bandwidth Table of Services

The **bandwidth** of a signal determines the frequency range it occupies in the spectrum.

Service Frequency Band Remarks
AM Radio 530 kHz – 1600 kHz Long-range, affected by atmospheric conditions
FM Radio 88 MHz – 108 MHz Better sound quality, short-range
Television Broadcast 54 MHz – 806 MHz VHF and UHF bands
Mobile Communication 800 MHz – 5 GHz Includes 3G, 4G, and 5G services
Satellite Communication 1 GHz – 40 GHz Used for global communication

Antenna

An antenna is a device that transmits and receives electromagnetic waves.

✅ Characteristics of an Antenna:
  • Radiation Pattern: Defines how signals are emitted.
  • Gain: Measure of how efficiently an antenna directs energy.
  • Polarization: Orientation of the electromagnetic wave.
✅ Types of Antennas:
  • Dipole Antenna: Used for radio transmission.
  • Parabolic Antenna: Used for satellite communication.
  • Yagi-Uda Antenna: Used in television reception.

Propagation of Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic waves propagate through different modes based on frequency and medium.

✅ Modes of Propagation:
  • Ground Wave Propagation: Used in AM radio, follows Earth's surface.
  • Sky Wave Propagation: Used in shortwave radio, reflects off the ionosphere.
  • Space Wave Propagation: Used in TV, FM radio, and satellite communication.

Earth's Atmosphere

The Earth's atmosphere is divided into different layers based on temperature variation.

✅ Layers of Atmosphere:
  • Troposphere: 0 - 12 km, weather phenomena occur here.
  • Stratosphere: 12 - 50 km, contains the ozone layer.
  • Mesosphere: 50 - 85 km, meteors burn up here.
  • Ionosphere: 85 - 600 km, contains charged particles, reflects radio waves.

Propagation of Radio Waves

Radio waves propagate through different methods based on frequency and atmospheric conditions.

✅ Types of Radio Wave Propagation:
  • Ground Wave (Surface Wave) Propagation:
    • Low-frequency waves travel along Earth's surface.
    • Used for AM radio transmission.
  • Sky Wave Propagation:
    • Waves reflect off the ionosphere back to Earth.
    • Used for long-distance communication.
  • Space Wave Propagation:
    • Waves travel in a straight line.
    • Used in FM radio, TV, and satellite communication.
