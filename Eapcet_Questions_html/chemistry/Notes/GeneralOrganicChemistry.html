Few Important Reactions

Wurtz Reaction

2 R–X + 2 Na → R–R + 2 NaX

Example:
2 C₂H₅Br
(Bromoethane)
+
2 Na
(Sodium)
→
C₄H₁₀
(Butane)
+
2 NaBr
(Sodium Bromide)

Condition: Dry Ether

Fittig Reaction

2 Ar–X + 2 Na → Ar–Ar + 2 NaX

Example:
2 C₆H₅Br
(Bromobenzene)
+
2 Na
(Sodium)
→
C₆H₅–C₆H₅
(Biphenyl)
+
2 NaBr
(Sodium Bromide)

Condition: Dry Ether

Wurtz-Fittig Reaction

Ar–X + R–X + 2 Na → Ar–R + 2 NaX

Example:
C₆H₅Br
(Bromobenzene)
+
CH₃Br
(Methyl bromide)
+
2 Na
(Sodium)
→
C₆H₅CH₃
(Toluene)
+
2 NaBr
(Sodium Bromide)

Condition: Dry Ether

Friedel-Crafts Alkylation

Ar–H + R–X → Ar–R + HX

Example:
C₆H₆
(Benzene)
+
CH₃Cl
(Methyl chloride)
→
C₆H₅CH₃
(Toluene)
+
HCl
(Byproduct)

Condition: Anhydrous AlCl3 (Catalyst)

Friedel-Crafts Acylation

Ar–H + RCOCl → Ar–CO–R + HCl

Example:
C₆H₆
(Benzene)
+
CH₃COCl
(Acetyl chloride)
→
C₆H₅COCH₃
(Acetophenone)
+
HCl
(Hydrogen chloride)

Condition: Anhydrous AlCl3 (Catalyst)

Decarboxylation Reaction

R–COONa → R–H + Na₂CO₃

Example:
CH₃COONa
(Sodium acetate)
→
CH₄
(Methane)
+
Na₂CO₃

Condition: Heat or catalyst like soda lime(CaO)

Hydrogenation of Unsaturated Compounds

R–CH=CH–R + H₂ → R–CH₂–CH₂–R

Example:
C₂H₄
(Ethene)
+
H₂
(Hydrogen)
→
C₂H₆
(Ethane)

Condition: Catalyst (e.g., Pt, Pd, or Ni) and Heat

Combustion of Hydrocarbons

CxHy + (x + y/4)O2 → xCO2 + (y/2)H2O

Example:
CH₄
(Methane)
+
2 O₂
(Oxygen)

CO₂
(Carbon dioxide)

2 H₂O
(Water)

Condition: Sufficient Oxygen and Heat

CH₃CH=CH₂
(Butene)
+
HBr

Condition: Strong Base (e.g., KOH, NaOH) in Alcohol
