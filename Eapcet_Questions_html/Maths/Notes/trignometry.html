Trigonometric Functions

Measurement of Angles

An angle is the figure formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint, called the vertex.

Angles can be measured in different units: Degrees, Radians, and Gradians.

Relationship Between Degree, Radian, and Gradian

There are three standard units of angle measurement:

  • Degree (°) - A full revolution is 360°.
  • Radian (rad) - The angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc whose length is equal to the radius.
  • Gradian (grad) - A full revolution is divided into 400 gradians.

Conversion formulas:

\[ 1 \text{ radian} = \frac{180}{\pi} \text{ degrees} \]

\[ 1 \text{ degree} = \frac{\pi}{180} \text{ radians} \]

\[ 1 \text{ gradian} = \frac{9}{10} \text{ degrees} \]

\[ 1 \text{ degree} = \frac{10}{9} \text{ gradians} \]

Thus, the relationship between them is:

\[ 180^\circ = \pi \text{ rad} = 200 \text{ gradians} \]

Trigonometric Ratios

Trigonometric ratios relate the angles of a right-angled triangle to the lengths of its sides.

For a right-angled triangle with:

  • Hypotenuse (h): The longest side opposite the right angle.
  • Opposite side (o): The side opposite to the given angle.
  • Adjacent side (a): The side next to the given angle.

Basic Trigonometric Ratios:

Ratio Formula
\(\sin \theta\) \(\frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{o}{h}\)
\(\cos \theta\) \(\frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{a}{h}\)
\(\tan \theta\) \(\frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{o}{a}\)
\(\csc \theta\) \(\frac{\text{Hypotenuse}}{\text{Opposite}} = \frac{h}{o}\)
\(\sec \theta\) \(\frac{\text{Hypotenuse}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{h}{a}\)
\(\cot \theta\) \(\frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Opposite}} = \frac{a}{o}\)

Important Identities:

  • \(\tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}\)
  • \(\cot \theta = \frac{1}{\tan \theta}\)
  • \(\csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta}\)
  • \(\sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta}\)

Trigonometric Functions of Standard Angles (0° to 90°)

Trigonometric functions for standard angles (0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°) are widely used in mathematics and physics.

Trigonometric Table:

\(\theta\) \(0^\circ\) \(30^\circ\) \(45^\circ\) \(60^\circ\) \(90^\circ\)
\(\sin \theta\) 0 \(\frac{1}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\) \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) 1
\(\cos \theta\) 1 \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\) \(\frac{1}{2}\) 0
\(\tan \theta\) 0 \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) 1 \(\sqrt{3}\) \(\infty\)
\(\csc \theta\) \(\infty\) 2 \(\sqrt{2}\) \(\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\) 1
\(\sec \theta\) 1 \(\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\) \(\sqrt{2}\) 2 \(\infty\)
\(\cot \theta\) \(\infty\) \(\sqrt{3}\) 1 \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) 0

Key Observations:

  • As \(\theta\) increases from \(0^\circ\) to \(90^\circ\), \(\sin \theta\) increases, and \(\cos \theta\) decreases.
  • \(\tan 90^\circ\) and \(\sec 90^\circ\) are undefined (\(\infty\)).
  • \(\csc 0^\circ\) and \(\cot 0^\circ\) are also undefined (\(\infty\)).

Pythagorean Identities

  • \[ \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \]
  • \[ 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \]
  • \[ 1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta \]

Compound Angle Formulae

Formula Expression
\(\sin(A + B)\) \(\sin A \cos B + \cos A \sin B\)
\(\sin(A - B)\) \(\sin A \cos B - \cos A \sin B\)
\(\cos(A + B)\) \(\cos A \cos B - \sin A \sin B\)
\(\cos(A - B)\) \(\cos A \cos B + \sin A \sin B\)
\(\tan(A + B)\) \(\frac{\tan A + \tan B}{1 - \tan A \tan B}\)
\(\tan(A - B)\) \(\frac{\tan A - \tan B}{1 + \tan A \tan B}\)
\(\cot(A + B)\) \(\frac{\cot A \cot B - 1}{\cot A + \cot B}\)
\(\cot(A - B)\) \(\frac{\cot A \cot B + 1}{\cot B - \cot A}\)

Transformation Formulae

Sum to Product Identities
Formula Expression
\(\sin A + \sin B\) \(2 \sin \frac{A+B}{2} \cos \frac{A-B}{2}\)
\(\sin A - \sin B\) \(2 \cos \frac{A+B}{2} \sin \frac{A-B}{2}\)
\(\cos A + \cos B\) \(2 \cos \frac{A+B}{2} \cos \frac{A-B}{2}\)
\(\cos A - \cos B\) \(-2 \sin \frac{A+B}{2} \sin \frac{A-B}{2}\)
Product to Sum Identities
Formula Expression
\(\sin A \sin B\) \(\frac{1}{2} [\cos (A - B) - \cos (A + B)]\)
\(\cos A \cos B\) \(\frac{1}{2} [\cos (A - B) + \cos (A + B)]\)
\(\sin A \cos B\) \(\frac{1}{2} [\sin (A + B) + \sin (A - B)]\)
\(\cos A \sin B\) \(\frac{1}{2} [\sin (A + B) - \sin (A - B)]\)

Multiple Angle Formulae

Double Angle Formulae
Formula Expression
\(\sin 2A\) \(2 \sin A \cos A\)
\(\cos 2A\) \(\cos^2 A - \sin^2 A = 2\cos^2 A - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2 A\)
\(\tan 2A\) \(\frac{2\tan A}{1 - \tan^2 A}\)
Triple Angle Formulae
Formula Expression
\(\sin 3A\) \(3 \sin A - 4 \sin^3 A\)
\(\cos 3A\) \(4 \cos^3 A - 3 \cos A\)
\(\tan 3A\) \(\frac{3\tan A - \tan^3 A}{1 - 3\tan^2 A}\)

Sub-Multiple Angle Formulae

Formula Expression
\(\sin \frac{A}{2}\) \(\pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos A}{2}}\)
\(\cos \frac{A}{2}\) \(\pm \sqrt{\frac{1 + \cos A}{2}}\)
\(\tan \frac{A}{2}\) \(\pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos A}{1 + \cos A}} = \frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A} = \frac{1 - \cos A}{\sin A}\)
